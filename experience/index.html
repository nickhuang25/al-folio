2019 - Present

AV Machine Learning Research

ML Research Assistant

My research team and I have worked to develop generators for images of terrain. To train the model, we first utilized a webscraper to harvest images of different types of terrain from Google images. Using these images, we trained a convolutional neural network to classify an icy road from other types of terrain. From there, we tuned a generative adversarial network to produce new images of icy roads, effectively testing the accuracy of the CNN. I am currently working on a novel approach to apply generic feature transfer (summer to winter) optimized for few shot learning to a small input dataset of images. I'm currently experimenting with cycle-consistency loss and PatchNCE loss to preserve the input feature mapping while training towards a target domain, while utilizing a pyramid GAN structure to facilitate few-shot learning.
